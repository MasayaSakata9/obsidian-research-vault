## Final Verified Summary

- **Title:** Physics-constrained machine learning for electrodynamics without gauge ambiguity based on Fourier transformed Maxwell's equations

- **One-paragraph summary:** Leon & Scheinker (2024, Scientific Reports) propose the Fourier–Helmholtz–Maxwell neural operator method, which hard-encodes Gauss's laws (for both E and B), Faraday's law, and the longitudinal Ampère–Maxwell constraint (in Fourier space, assuming the continuity equation) directly into the network architecture. An encoder–decoder network predicts the transverse components of the Fourier-transformed vector potential Ā⊥(k,t), from which E and B fields are derived without gauge ambiguity. The approach was validated on two intense relativistic charged particle beam simulations, where a U-Net architecture outperformed other tested architectures in speed, accuracy, and generalization. The authors claim orders-of-magnitude speedup over conventional simulations and successful retraining on unseen data in as few as 20 epochs. No numerical error metrics or specific baseline architectures are reported in the abstract. Code availability is unconfirmed.

- **Usefulness for the current research:** **Medium — indirect inspiration only.** The hard-constraint design philosophy and Fourier-space constraint enforcement are conceptually valuable for improving the 2D FNO baseline for indoor WiFi prediction. However, the physical domain (time-dependent beam electrodynamics vs. steady-state Helmholtz with complex boundaries) is too different for direct method transfer. The "20 epochs" and "orders of magnitude faster" claims cannot be assumed to generalize to the WiFi surrogate problem.

- **Implementation implication:** If adapting this approach, the Fourier-space hard constraints would need to be reformulated for the steady-state Helmholtz equation (∇·D=0 in source-free regions, ∇×E=0 in static limit). The U-Net architecture itself is readily available. Expected cost: **Medium** — constraint reformulation is the main work.
